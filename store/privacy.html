Privacy Policy

Last updated 23 July 2026

This page is a pre-launch preview for Skippidy Skip, operated by Misaligned Games ("we", "us"). It helps us gauge interest in upcoming games before release. Questions: hello@misaligned.games.

What we collect

  • Anonymous usage events — page views and button taps, with a random session identifier (not tied to your identity).
  • Campaign attribution — the ad source/campaign parameters (UTM) and referrer in the link you arrived from.
  • Email address — only if you choose to submit it to be notified at launch.

Why

To measure which game concepts and ads people respond to, and to notify interested people when a game launches. We do not build advertising profiles from this data.

Sharing

We do not sell your data. Event data is stored with our infrastructure provider (Supabase) solely to produce aggregate interest metrics.

If you install the Android app

Once Skippidy Skip is released and you install it from an app store, the app additionally uses:

  • PostHog analytics (EU-hosted) — anonymous product-analytics events, an anonymous device identifier, and approximate (country-level) location derived from your IP address. Not tied to your name or email; we do not sell it.
  • AppsFlyer install attribution — your device’s advertising ID (GAID) and basic install/app-open events, to measure which ad campaigns lead to installs (the app declares the com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID permission). AppsFlyer processes this on our behalf; aggregate campaign results are shared with our advertising partners (e.g. AppLovin). We do not use it to build an advertising profile of you. You can reset or delete your advertising ID in Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.

Data is encrypted in transit. On request we delete analytics and attribution data associated with your device (see below).

Your choices

To access or delete any data associated with you (including an email you submitted), email hello@misaligned.games. So we can find your data, include your device’s advertising ID (Android Settings → Privacy → Ads — the ID is shown at the bottom of that screen): attribution data is keyed to it and we will delete it via our provider. Gameplay analytics are stored under a random in-app identifier that is not linked to your name, email or advertising ID — we cannot look it up from the outside, which also means it cannot be used to identify you; it is deleted automatically per our retention schedule. You can also sever attribution yourself at any time by resetting or deleting your advertising ID in the same Android settings screen.
